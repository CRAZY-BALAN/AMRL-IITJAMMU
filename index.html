About AMRL

Where Mechanics Meets Intelligence

The Additive Manufacturing & Research Laboratory (AMRL) at IIT Jammu is dedicated to pioneering research and technological advancements in additive manufacturing, smart materials, and advanced mechanical systems. The laboratory focuses on developing innovative manufacturing solutions through cutting-edge design, simulation, and fabrication technologies.

AMRL brings together interdisciplinary expertise in mechanical engineering, materials science, computational design, and digital manufacturing to address real-world industrial and research challenges. The laboratory aims to drive innovation in sustainable manufacturing, lightweight structures, lattice optimization, and next-generation production systems.

Design, Development & Validation of Data-Driven Functionally Graded TPMS Lattice Structure-Based Prostheses Using Multi-Material Additive Manufacturing
Ongoing (PI)
Agency
ICMR, India
Period
09/2025 – 09/2028
This project focuses on developing next-generation prosthetic limbs using functionally graded TPMS (Triply Periodic Minimal Surface) lattice structures. By leveraging data-driven design methodologies and multi-material additive manufacturing, the research aims to create patient-specific prostheses that mimic natural bone mechanical behavior, offering superior comfort and durability. The integration of machine learning models with topology optimization enables spatially varying porosity and stiffness profiles within the prosthetic socket, significantly improving osseointegration and load distribution.
TPMS Prosthetics Multi-Material AM Data-Driven Design
Selective Laser Polymerization for Thermoset-Based Dental Implants
Ongoing (PI)
Agency
DST TDP, Dept. of Science & Technology, India
Period
03/2023 – 03/2026
This project investigates selective laser polymerization (SLP) as a novel additive manufacturing technique for producing thermoset-based dental implants with high dimensional accuracy and mechanical integrity. The research develops custom photopolymer resin formulations with bio-compatible properties suitable for intraoral environments. Process parameter optimization through design of experiments (DoE) ensures superior surface finish, depth-of-cure uniformity, and long-term biocompatibility verified through in-vitro cell studies.
Dental Implants SLP Thermoset Resins
Development and Validation of Biomimetic 3D Printed Dental Implant for Early Osseointegration
Ongoing (CO-PI)
Agency
ICMR, IIT AIIMS, IIIM Jammu
Period
2024 – 2027
This collaborative project between IIT Jammu, AIIMS, and IIIM Jammu focuses on designing biomimetic dental implants via 3D printing that promote early osseointegration. Surface micro-architecture inspired by natural bone hierarchical structures is fabricated using metal AM, significantly reducing the osseointegration timeline. The project combines in-vitro biological validation with finite element analysis to optimize implant geometry, surface porosity, and material composition for accelerated bone-implant bonding.
Biomimetics Dental Implant Osseointegration
Annular Laser Beam Based Direct Metal Deposition of Aluminium Matrix Reinforced Composites
Ongoing (CO-PI)
Agency
SERB (CRG), India
Period
2023 – 2026
This project explores the use of annular (ring-shaped) laser beams for Direct Energy Deposition (DED) of aluminium matrix composite (AMC) materials reinforced with ceramic particles. The annular beam profile provides a unique thermal gradient that reduces porosity and improves reinforcement particle distribution within the matrix. Research outcomes aim to establish process windows and microstructure-property correlations for high-performance lightweight AMC components applicable to aerospace and automotive industries.
Metal DED Aluminium Matrix Composites
Remanufacturing of Aero-engine Parts Using Additive Manufacturing
Ongoing (PI)
Agency
CoPT – DRDO, India
Period
3 Years
This defense-funded project develops AM-based remanufacturing strategies for critical aero-engine components such as turbine blades and combustion chamber liners. Using DED and laser cladding, worn or damaged parts are restored to OEM specifications or better. The project addresses material compatibility, inter-layer bonding integrity, residual stress management, and non-destructive evaluation protocols to qualify remanufactured parts for military and civilian aerospace applications.
Aerospace Remanufacturing DED / Laser Cladding
ABD 900 Superalloy for Aero Engine Applications Using Additive Manufacturing
Ongoing (CO-PI)
Agency
ARDB – DRDO, India
Period
3 Years
This project investigates the selective laser melting (SLM) processing of ABD-900 AM, a newly developed nickel-based superalloy specifically designed for additive manufacturing. The research establishes optimal process parameters to achieve dense, crack-free microstructures with superior high-temperature mechanical properties. Detailed characterization of microstructure, texture, oxidation resistance, and fatigue behavior is conducted to qualify ABD-900 components for next-generation gas turbine aero-engine applications under extreme operating conditions.
Superalloy SLM Aero Engine

Selective Laser Sintering of Advanced Polymer Composites
Completed (PI)
Agency
SEED Grant, IIT Jammu
Period
05/2021 – 05/2025
This foundational project established SLS processing capabilities at IIT Jammu for advanced polymer composites including PA12-glass bead, PA12-carbon fibre, and novel bio-filled composites. Comprehensive process parameter optimization studies were conducted, resulting in parts with improved mechanical properties, reduced porosity, and better surface quality. The project produced 20+ journal publications and established the material database for SLS-based research at AMRL.
SLS Polymer Composite PA12
3D Printed Sustainable Candle Moulds
Completed (PI)
Agency
Unnat Bharat Abhiyan, Ministry of Education, Govt. of India
Period
03/2024 – 03/2025
A socially impactful project under the Unnat Bharat Abhiyan scheme, this initiative developed low-cost 3D printed candle mould solutions for rural artisans and self-help groups in Jammu & Kashmir. Custom FDM-printed moulds enabled artisans to produce complex candle shapes previously unachievable with traditional methods. The project trained 50+ beneficiaries and established a replicable rural manufacturing model combining digital design with affordable fabrication.
Sustainable Design FDM Rural Manufacturing
Assessment of 3D Composite Fabrication in Indian Industry
Completed (PI)
Agency
A2k+ Program Grant, DSIR, India
Period
03/2022 – 05/2024
This national-level industrial assessment project surveyed and evaluated the readiness of Indian manufacturing industries to adopt 3D composite fabrication technologies (TRL-6 level). The study covered 200+ industries across sectors including aerospace, automotive, defense, and consumer goods. Recommendations were provided to government bodies on technology transfer pathways, skill development needs, and policy interventions required to accelerate AM adoption in India's industrial ecosystem.
Composite Fabrication Industrial Assessment TRL-6
Functionally Graded 3D Printed Patient Comfort Socket Prosthetics
Completed (PI)
Agency
Start-up Grant, SERB, India
Period
12/2021 – 12/2023
This SERB-funded startup grant project developed functionally graded prosthetic sockets using multi-material FDM printing, enabling spatially controlled stiffness distribution from rigid structural zones to flexible patient-interface regions. Patient-specific designs derived from 3D scanning and topology optimization algorithms resulted in clinically tested prototypes with significantly improved comfort scores. Clinical trials were conducted in collaboration with orthopedic centers, with outcomes published in leading biomedical engineering journals.
Socket Prosthetics Topology Optimization Functionally Graded
Technological Gap Analysis for Woven Metallic and Glass Fibre Measuring Tapes
Completed (PI)
Agency
Bureau of Indian Standards (BIS), India
Period
07/2024 – 02/2025
Commissioned by the Bureau of Indian Standards, this project conducted a systematic technological gap analysis of Indian manufacturing capabilities for woven metallic and glass fibre measuring tapes against international standards. Material characterization (tensile strength, thermal coefficient, UV stability), manufacturing process audit, and metrological performance evaluation were performed across multiple domestic manufacturers. Findings directly informed revision of BIS standards IS 1270 and IS 4491 to align with ISO benchmarks.
Glass Fibre BIS Standards Material Analysis
SLM Fabrication of Haynes-230 Nickel-Base Superalloy
Completed (CO-PI)
Agency
CARS Grant, DRDO, India
Period
02/2022 – 03/2023
This DRDO-funded CARS grant project established SLM process parameters for Haynes-230, a Ni-W-Cr-based high-temperature superalloy used in combustion chambers and gas turbine components. The study investigated the effect of laser power, scan speed, hatch spacing, and layer thickness on relative density, hardness, and high-temperature tensile behavior. Electron backscatter diffraction (EBSD) was used to characterize crystallographic texture, and the results provided a foundational dataset for qualification of SLM-produced Haynes-230 in defense applications.
SLM Nickel Superalloy Haynes-230

Lab Director & PI
Dr. Raj Kumar Velu
Assistant Professor, Mechanical Engineering · IIT Jammu
PhD · Auckland University of Technology , New Zealand |  Postdoctoral Researcher · Singapore University of Technology and Design(SUTD), Singapore
Dr. Raj Kumar Velu is the founder and Principal Investigator of AMRL. His research spans Additive Manufacturing (SLS, SLM, FDM, DED), functionally graded and TPMS lattice structures, polymer & metal composite fabrication, topology optimisation, and patient-specific biomedical devices — including prosthetics and dental implants. He has authored 40+ peer-reviewed publications and leads high-value sponsored projects funded by ICMR, DST, SERB, and DRDO, with a cumulative funding of over ₹7 Crore.
